For my writing class, I had to watch Anthony Chen’s Haze (2008), a film that explores the fears and desires that exist within the adolescent experience. Throughout the film, not much happens as the boy and girl spontaneously spend their afternoon unaccompanied in the boy’s home. However, under their mundane conversations and nuanced gestures, the two character’s serious thoughts and unyielding values silently clash. They act behind nonchalant facades, seeming to fear commitment, hesitate confession or have trouble resolving uncertain feelings.

How an adolescent deals with these subverted emotions is what makes growing up a unique experience. Taking the ideas that Chen has conveyed through his short film, I was inspired to create an editorial that would artistically and truthfully portray my emotional journey throughout the past few years. The central focus is on what I’ve known, but always (possibly purposely) hidden.
