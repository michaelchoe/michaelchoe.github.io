As the art director for Thread Magazine, I will be primarily responsible for creating and directing the layout of the spring 2018 issue. However, throughout the semester I will have other projects to do. I have taken the initiative to create an electronic newsletter and made a booklet in preparation for Cornell's annual fashion showcase.

THREAD SS18: The Technology Issue



In the 12th issue, Thread’s creatives wanted to open discussion about how technology has influenced the ways in which we interact with the self and others. The creative director pinpointed five concepts in which the technology age has had great impact: the social implications of the nude; how people view and find romance; the means through which humans communicate with others; the advancement of photography techniques and how each photographical medium has its own respective sentimental value; the creation of VR and how virtual realities are becoming as real as they are virtual.

I made sure to make every spread have it’s own unique voice, but intertwined all five into a magazine that is cohesive and effectively communicates the message that the creative director hoped to pass onto readers. CREATIVES Booklet





The main purpose of this booklet is to give attendees of the Cornell Fashion Show a better understanding of the fashion community and classes at Cornell University. The booklet highlights thirteen seniors who have made a significant impact on the fashion/creative community at Cornell during their four years on campus.

At the day of the runway show, around 2000 people will see this booklet; thus, it is very important to give a visually strong impression of the people featured and Cornell's creatives as a whole. Therefore, through the use a monochromatic, dark color scheme along with edgy details, I aimed to bring out the "coolness" within these people.

All photos were taken by Thread Magazine's photography director, Savanna Lim
